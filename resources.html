Where to go next.

A short reading list for anyone starting from anywhere. Books, essays, and conversations that go deeper than ten minutes a day.

01 · For beginners

Starting out.

The best places to begin if you're new to the Talmud, or new to Jewish learning altogether.

Book · 2023
How the Talmud Can Change Your Life.
by Liel Leibovitz
The book we're sending every Founding 100 member. A warm, essayistic case for the Talmud's practical wisdom — and why it still matters. Ten short chapters, one big argument.
Buy the book ↗
Reference · Koren
The Gemara Card.
Koren Publishers
A tri-fold pocket reference for reading a page of Talmud — vocabulary, abbreviations, key phrases, sages. The single most useful cheat sheet for a new learner. Also part of the Founding 100 perk.
See on Koren ↗
Book · Classic intro
The Essential Talmud.
by Rabbi Adin Steinsaltz
The book many rabbis assign as the first read on the Talmud. What the Talmud is, how it's structured, and how to think with it. Steinsaltz's clearest, most accessible short introduction.
Read more ↗
Book · Deep intro
The Talmud: A Biography.
by Barry Scott Wimpfheimer
A short cultural history of the book itself — how it was assembled, how it was banned and burned, how it became the anchor of Jewish thought for 1,500 years. Great context before you open a page.
Read more ↗
